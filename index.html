Projects

E-learning Self-Paced Model on Exceptional Customer Service

I designed and developed this corporate training course on Exceptional Customer Service Skills from the ground up. This interactive micro course incorporates adult learning theories, instructional design best practices, and key corporate training principles. I integrated branching scenarios, hands-on activities, and various interactive elements to drive engagement and ensure learning retention.

Show More Details

Audience: Entry- to associate-level customer service representatives at Chi-Chack LLC, focused on building foundational skills to confidently handle customer issues, complaints, and escalations.

Responsibilities: Instructional Design, E-learning Development, Visual Design, Storyboarding, SME Collaboration, Mockups, and Prototypes.

Tools Used: Articulate Storyline, Adobe Photoshop, Audacity, Vyond, Google Docs (for collaborative storyboarding), Microsoft PowerPoint (for visual mockups), and Moodle LMS for tracking learner progress.

Overview

As Chi-Chack LLC's language instructional designer, I recognized an opportunity to improve the effectiveness of their existing customer service training, which was time-consuming and largely video-based. After a conversation with a senior customer service representative, I offered to develop a more streamlined, scenario-based eLearning course that could deliver real impact in less time. This resulted in a self-paced training program that used real-world scenarios to help associates master key customer service skills efficiently.

Action Map

To guide the development of this course, I collaborated with the senior SME to create an action map. This helped pinpoint five critical actions that associates needed to improve on, such as resolving customer complaints, de-escalating situations, and properly escalating cases. These actions formed the foundation of the eLearning course, ensuring that every scenario was tied to real-world applications that aligned with business objectives.

Full Development

Developing this course required continuous collaboration with the SME and feedback from the customer service team. I followed the ADDIE model throughout the project, starting with analysis and working through design, development, implementation, and evaluation.

  • Collaborated with SMEs to identify the five critical customer service actions required to improve job performance.
  • Created a storyboard and developed visual mockups to refine the course layout and flow, ensuring alignment with the company’s brand.
  • Developed the course using Articulate Storyline, combining multimedia elements like interactive quizzes, videos, and audio narration to ensure learner engagement.
  • Conducted iterative testing with user groups to improve content based on feedback.

Storyboarding

I developed a text-based storyboard that utilized the five critical actions from the action map. These actions were built into branching scenarios, with the correct answers as key actions and common mistakes as distractors. I worked closely with the SME to ensure each decision had realistic consequences, ensuring the training reflected real-life customer service interactions.

Visual Mockups

After receiving approval on the storyboard, I moved to the visual design phase. Using Adobe XD, I developed mockups of the course, including custom progress bars, buttons, and character design. The visuals aligned with the company’s branding and offered a clean, professional interface. This step was crucial in allowing for early feedback before full development began in Articulate Storyline.

Interactive Prototype

Before moving into full development, I created an interactive prototype in Articulate Storyline, focusing on the first two scenarios. This allowed me to test the course’s functionality, including custom animations and branching scenarios, gathering feedback from the SME and adjusting based on user interaction.

Results

The course increased learner engagement by 40%, with LMS tracking showing high interaction rates. Post-training feedback indicated a 25% improvement in customer satisfaction, and new associates reported feeling more confident in handling difficult customer interactions. This contributed to a measurable improvement in customer retention and reduced escalations.

Takeaways

This project demonstrated the importance of scenario-based learning and how aligning training with real-world tasks can drive both learner engagement and business results. By focusing on core performance challenges and integrating them into the course design, I was able to deliver a solution that met both the learner’s needs and the company’s objectives.

VoiceReal – Web App for Language Acquisition

I coded and programmed this intuitive and user-friendly web app using using a mix of programming languages. VoiceReal can read multiple languages with both male and female voices, allowing users to instantly listen to any text. I incorporated features that enable users to adjust the pitch and speed of the voice to match their preferences.

Show More Details

I developed VoiceReal as part of a larger proposal writing and marketing strategy to showcase the role of technology in language acquisition, supporting students at the University of Montana’s Defense Critical Language and Culture Program (DCLCP).

Responsibilities: Web Development, API Integration, Programming in JavaScript/CSS/HTML, Collaboration with Cross-Functional Teams, Conference Presentation, Grant Writing Support.

Tools Used: JavaScript, CSS, HTML, API integration for text-to-speech functionalities, GitHub for version control.

Process

  • I worked with cross-functional teams of language instructors, students, and managers to define the app's core features and functionalities.
  • I programmed the app using JavaScript, CSS, and HTML, with real-time text-to-speech functionalities integrated through external APIs.
  • I collaborated with stakeholders to ensure the app's design was both user-friendly and capable of supporting multiple languages.
  • I presented the app at a conference, which helped secure additional grant funding for the University of Montana’s DCLCP.

Results

VoiceReal played a key role in securing additional grant funding for the University of Montana’s DCLCP, allowing the program to expand its technological resources. I received positive feedback during the conference presentation, where the app demonstrated the University’s innovative approach to language acquisition. VoiceReal continues to support reading and listening comprehension in multiple languages, with ongoing improvements based on user feedback. Students, particularly those in the early stages of learning new languages, have found it extremely helpful in building their skills. Instructors also actively use it in classrooms to teach reading and listening comprehension, making it an essential tool for language acquisition.

Takeaways

This project highlighted the value of integrating technology into language learning and the importance of collaboration across functional teams. By designing VoiceReal, an adaptable tool, I contributed to both the learning experience and the strategic goals of the DCLCP. The success of this project reinforced the need to stay agile and responsive to user needs, while leveraging technology to create impactful learning solutions.

Virtual Keyboard with Interactive Features

I coded and programmed this this responsive and user-friendly virtual keyboard from the ground up with a rich set of features and a Canva-like interface. This tool supports multiple languages and requires no authentication. Using JavaScript, CSS, and HTML, I created an interactive experience that allows users to draw, erase, and engage with the keyboard in versatile ways.

Show More Details

Audience: The virtual keyboard is designed for users looking for a flexible, interactive tool to write in multiple languages without needing authentication, including educators, language learners, and professionals who need a versatile digital tool for language input.

Responsibilities: Front-End Development, UI/UX Design, JavaScript Programming, API Integration, Responsive Design, Feature Development.

Tools Used: JavaScript, CSS, HTML, Canvas API for drawing features, GitHub for version control.

Overview

I coded and programmed this app to support online language instruction by addressing the need for interactivity, especially for those learning a new language. Before this app, instructors often asked students to buy keyboard alphabet stickers and manually install their target language on devices to practice writing. I volunteered to develop a solution that combined all the keyboards in one place, making it easier for learners to access and practice multiple languages. Working with cross-functional teams and students, I iterated and refined the app through extensive testing.

Full Development

This project was built using JavaScript, CSS, and HTML, with the Canvas API integrated to enable drawing and erasing functionality. The tool's interface was designed to be intuitive and simple while supporting complex functionalities like multilingual input, adjustable layouts, and a no-authentication-required model to keep the user experience seamless.

I worked closely with instructors and students during the design and testing phases to ensure the app met educational needs. The project went through several iterations to improve accessibility and responsiveness. Feedback from users helped refine the interactive elements, making the tool more user-friendly and adaptable.

Action Maps

While this project was more technical, I applied principles of user-centered design. I identified key functionalities that directly impacted user satisfaction, such as switching between languages seamlessly, integrating interactive drawing and erasing features, and ensuring the tool was responsive on both desktop and mobile devices.

Process

  • Identified key features such as drawing, erasing, and multilingual support to enhance the tool's versatility.
  • Developed the app using JavaScript, CSS, and HTML, with heavy use of the Canvas API to handle interactive drawing features.
  • Designed the UI to resemble the clean, intuitive style of Canva, ensuring simplicity and engagement.
  • Tested the app across various devices to ensure responsiveness and ease of use for all types of users.

Results

The virtual keyboard received positive feedback for its flexibility and ease of use. It was especially appreciated by students in the early stages of language learning, as it eliminated the barriers of installing language packs or using external keyboards. Instructors also used it in classrooms to support reading and writing exercises, making it a valuable tool for teaching.

Takeaways

This project demonstrated the importance of building responsive, accessible tools that cater to real user needs. The integration of interactive features such as drawing and erasing allowed for greater flexibility and engagement, making the tool practical for both independent learners and classroom settings. It reinforced the value of functionality, design, and user experience when developing educational tools.
