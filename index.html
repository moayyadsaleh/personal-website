Projects

E-learning Course on Exceptional Customer Service

I designed and developed this corporate training course on Exceptional Customer Service Skills from the ground up. This interactive course incorporates adult learning theories, instructional design best practices, and key corporate training principles. I integrated branching scenarios, hands-on activities, and various interactive elements to drive engagement and ensure learning retention.

Show More Details

Audience: Entry- to associate-level customer service representatives at Chi-Chack LLC(my past employer), focusing on mastering key skills to handle customer concerns, complaints, and escalations with confidence.

Responsibilities: Instructional Design, E-learning Development, Visual Design, Storyboarding, SME Collaboration, Mockups, and Prototypes.

Tools Used: Articulate Storyline, Adobe Photoshop, Audacity, Vyond, Google Docs for storyboarding collaboration, Microsoft PowerPoint for visual mockups, and Moodle LMS for tracking learner progress.

Overview

While working as an instructional designer at Chi-Chack LLC, I noticed an opportunity to improve their existing customer service training, which was primarily video-based and time-consuming. After a conversation with a senior customer service rep, I volunteered to create a more efficient, scenario-driven eLearning course. This resulted in a self-paced model that uses real-life scenarios, helping associates build the skills they need in a fraction of the time.

Action Map

Collaborating closely with the senior SME, we built an action map to identify five key customer service behaviors that separate great customer service from good or standard service. These behaviors included not just de-escalating and escalating customer issues properly, but also going the extra mile in problem resolution, anticipating customer needs, and delivering personalized experiences. These critical actions formed the backbone of the course design, ensuring that every scenario demonstrated how to elevate performance from satisfactory to exceptional, aligned with real-world customer interactions.

Full Development

The project followed the ADDIE model, involving constant collaboration with the SME and feedback from customer service teams. I oversaw all phases, from analysis through to evaluation, ensuring the course met business goals while keeping learners engaged.

  • Worked with SMEs to pinpoint five key customer service skills necessary for better job performance.
  • Designed and storyboarded scenarios that mirrored real-life customer interactions to make the training as relevant as possible.
  • Developed the course in Articulate Storyline, integrating quizzes, interactive videos, and audio narration to enhance engagement.
  • Performed multiple rounds of user testing and iterations to refine content and improve the overall learning experience.

Storyboarding

I developed a text-based storyboard that utilized the five critical actions from the action map. These actions were built into branching scenarios, with the correct answers as key actions and common mistakes as distractors. I worked closely with the SME to ensure each decision had realistic consequences, ensuring the training reflected real-life customer service interactions.

Visual Mockups

After storyboarding was approved, I moved to visual design using Adobe XD, crafting a clean, branded interface. I designed custom elements like progress bars and navigation buttons, ensuring consistency with the company’s style guide.

Interactive Prototype

Before final development, I created an interactive prototype in Articulate Storyline. This allowed us to test the flow and functionality of the course, particularly focusing on the first two scenarios. Based on feedback, I made several adjustments to improve usability and engagement.

Results

The course boosted learner engagement by 40%, with LMS data reflecting high interaction rates. Post-training surveys revealed a 25% improvement in customer satisfaction scores, and associates reported feeling more confident in resolving complex customer issues. This led to a noticeable reduction in escalations and an improvement in overall customer retention.

Takeaways

This project reinforced the value of scenario-based learning, showing me firsthand how connecting training to real-world situations can significantly boost both learner engagement and business outcomes. By focusing on what truly makes customer service exceptional, and integrating those behaviors into realistic, relatable scenarios, I learned how powerful hands-on learning can be. I also gained a deeper understanding of how aligning the training content with core business challenges, like improving customer satisfaction and reducing escalations, can directly impact performance. This experience not only helped me grow as an instructional designer but also taught me how to create solutions that simultaneously meet learner needs and drive tangible business results.

VoiceReal – Web App for Language Acquisition

I coded and programmed this intuitive and user-friendly web app using using a mix of programming languages. VoiceReal can read multiple languages with both male and female voices, allowing users to instantly listen to any text. I incorporated features that enable users to adjust the pitch and speed of the voice to match their preferences.

Show More Details

I developed VoiceReal in response to a significant gap in language learning tools—there were no specialized apps that combined multiple languages in one platform specifically designed to help learners boost their reading and listening skills, especially those in the initial stages of language acquisition. As part of a larger proposal writing and marketing strategy, VoiceReal was created to showcase how technology can play a transformative role in language learning, supporting students at the University of Montana’s Defense Critical Language and Culture Program (DCLCP).

Responsibilities: Web Development, API Integration, Programming in JavaScript/CSS/HTML, Collaboration with Cross-Functional Teams, Conference Presentation, Grant Writing Support.

Tools Used: JavaScript, CSS, HTML, API integration for text-to-speech functionalities, GitHub for version control.

Process

  • I collaborated closely with cross-functional teams, including language instructors, students, and managers, to define the app's core features and functionalities. This involved gathering diverse input to ensure the app met both instructional goals and learner needs. Through multiple brainstorming sessions and feedback loops, we identified essential elements like real-time text-to-speech, multi-language support, and a user-friendly interface.
  • The app's development was built on a foundation of JavaScript, CSS, and HTML, which I used to program its structure and behavior. Integrating real-time text-to-speech functionalities via external APIs was a technical challenge, but it was essential for providing a seamless, interactive experience for users learning multiple languages. This experience deepened my understanding of API integration and its role in enhancing user interaction.
  • Throughout the design process, I worked closely with stakeholders, continuously refining the app’s interface to ensure it was intuitive and user-friendly. The app had to accommodate users with varying tech skills, so it was critical to design an interface that was simple yet robust. At the same time, supporting multiple languages presented challenges in terms of layout and functionality, which required creative solutions to ensure a smooth user experience.
  • I had the opportunity to present the app at a national conference, highlighting its innovative approach to language learning. This presentation not only demonstrated the app’s capabilities but also illustrated its potential to enhance language acquisition. The positive reception led to securing additional grant funding for the University of Montana’s DCLCP, enabling further development and expansion of the project.

Results

VoiceReal played a key role in securing additional grant funding for the University of Montana’s DCLCP, allowing the program to expand its technological resources. I received positive feedback during the conference presentation, where the app demonstrated the University’s innovative approach to language acquisition. VoiceReal continues to support reading and listening comprehension in multiple languages, with ongoing improvements based on user feedback. Students, particularly those in the early stages of learning new languages, have found it extremely helpful in building their skills. Instructors also actively use it in classrooms to teach reading and listening comprehension, making it an essential tool for language acquisition.

Takeaways

This project was a turning point in my understanding of how technology can truly transform language learning. Developing VoiceReal not only gave me the opportunity to blend my technical skills with my instructional design knowledge, but it also taught me the value of building adaptable, user-focused learning tools. Collaborating with cross-functional teams reinforced the importance of communication and teamwork in bringing a complex project like this to life. I learned how critical it is to stay agile—constantly gathering feedback and iterating to meet the evolving needs of learners. This experience also strengthened my belief that when technology is carefully integrated into educational design, it can create powerful, lasting learning experiences that align with both the learners' needs and institutional goals.

Virtual Keyboard with Interactive Features

I coded and programmed this this responsive and user-friendly virtual keyboard from the ground up with a rich set of features and a Canva-like interface. This tool supports multiple languages and requires no authentication. Using JavaScript, CSS, and HTML, I created an interactive experience that allows users to draw, erase, and engage with the keyboard in versatile ways.

Show More Details

Audience: The virtual keyboard is designed for users looking for a flexible, interactive tool to write in multiple languages without needing authentication, including educators, language learners, and professionals who need a versatile digital tool for language input.

Responsibilities: Front-End Development, UI/UX Design, JavaScript Programming, API Integration, Responsive Design, Feature Development.

Tools Used: JavaScript, CSS, HTML, Canvas API for drawing features, GitHub for version control.

Overview

I coded and programmed this app to support online language instruction by addressing the need for interactivity, especially for those learning a new language. Before this app, instructors often asked students to buy keyboard alphabet stickers and manually install their target language on devices to practice writing. I volunteered to develop a solution that combined all the keyboards in one place, making it easier for learners to access and practice multiple languages. Working with cross-functional teams and students, I iterated and refined the app through extensive testing.

Full Development

This project was built using JavaScript, CSS, and HTML, with the Canvas API integrated to enable drawing and erasing functionality. The tool's interface was designed to be intuitive and simple while supporting complex functionalities like multilingual input, adjustable layouts, and a no-authentication-required model to keep the user experience seamless.

I worked closely with instructors and students during the design and testing phases to ensure the app met educational needs. The project went through several iterations to improve accessibility and responsiveness. Feedback from users helped refine the interactive elements, making the tool more user-friendly and adaptable.

Action Maps

While this project was more technical, I applied principles of user-centered design. I identified key functionalities that directly impacted user satisfaction, such as switching between languages seamlessly, integrating interactive drawing and erasing features, and ensuring the tool was responsive on both desktop and mobile devices.

Process

  • Identified key features such as drawing, erasing, and multilingual support to enhance the tool's versatility.
  • Developed the app using JavaScript, CSS, and HTML, with heavy use of the Canvas API to handle interactive drawing features.
  • Designed the UI to resemble the clean, intuitive style of Canva, ensuring simplicity and engagement.
  • Tested the app across various devices to ensure responsiveness and ease of use for all types of users.

Results

The virtual keyboard received positive feedback for its flexibility and ease of use. It was especially appreciated by students in the early stages of language learning, as it eliminated the barriers of installing language packs or using external keyboards. Instructors also used it in classrooms to support reading and writing exercises, making it a valuable tool for teaching.

Takeaways

This project demonstrated the importance of building responsive, accessible tools that cater to real user needs. The integration of interactive features such as drawing and erasing allowed for greater flexibility and engagement, making the tool practical for both independent learners and classroom settings. It reinforced the value of functionality, design, and user experience when developing educational tools.
